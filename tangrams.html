Tangrams

Colourful shapes with over a billion combinations? you'll love Tangrams. Shapes comprise: two large triangles, two small triangles, one medium triangle, one parallelogram and one square.

Silhouette or outline only - geometrical shapes, letters, numbers, figures, boats, animals and objects.

Click the image to play!

Tangrams or Chin-Chiao Pan (intriguing seven piece puzzle) were created in Imperial China during the Tang Dynasty, and are thought to have travelled to Europe in the 19th century on trading ships.

A book published in China in 1815 by Shan-Chiao contains 374 puzzle patterns.

Were popular during World War 1 and became the most popular dissection puzzle in the world

Click to Learn more

Rules:

  • all tangram pieces (or tans) must be used
  • must lay flat
  • all must touch
  • none may overlap

Uses:

  • early psychological tests
  • teaching children mathematical concepts - early Chinese mathematicians manipulated geometric shapes in their problem solving.
