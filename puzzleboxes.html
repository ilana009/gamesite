Puzzle Boxes

Nothing compares to the feeling of holding a puzzle box in yours hands and judging the weight for what could be inside. Don't forget how to open it! (keep solutions hidden seperately) but if you're stuck and its NOT yours, the box is doing its job!

Originate from all over the world as souvineers during the 19th and early 20th centuries. They make great gifts for yourself or other puzzle lovers - even make your own.

Japanese Puzzle Box

Known as "himitsu-bako" (personal secret box), the earliest were created in the 19th century in Hakone, as tourist souvineers from the famous hot springs. Originally designed to hold trinkets but grew in size.

Features:

  • decorative wood panelling
  • sliding panels
  • secret compartments

Turkish Puzzle Box

Created as souvenirs for travelers from medieval caravanserais along the Silk Road to the Grand Bazaar of modern-day Istanbul. Traditionally made of Walnut or Juniper wood

Features:

  • decorative wood panelling
  • hidden key and lock
  • fabric lined interior

Cryptex Puzzle Box

Described as stone cylinder made of five marble disks stack together and fixed with delicate brass framwork in the The Da Vinci Code.

Features:

  • combination lock - words, initialism and anagrams
  • five rows of alphabet keys
  • hollow chamber
